Una Arepa por favor

What is Arepa?

Originally from South America, specifically Venezuela and Colombia, Arepas aresmall cornmeal-based corn cakesthat are cooked over the stove on a wire rack (Colombia) or lightly fried and baked (Venezuela). They can be eaten on their own, but you can also add fillings. Read on for instructions on how to make arepas with three different fillings.

Good to know !

Are you allergic to gluten? Well, since the Arepa is gluten-free, it is perfectly healthy for you.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups (240g) cornmeal
  • 2 cups (240 ml) of water
  • pinch of salt
  • oil

How to make Arepa

And how many types of Arepa are there?


Here we show you what we consider the most traditional and delicious recipe. But there are multiple types of Arepa, and they are:


  • Ground corn arepa
  • Rice arepa
  • Arepa of ocumo dough
  • Cassava dough arepa
  • Auyama dough arepa
  • Wheat arepa (Andean arepa)
  • Beetroot arepa (red arepa)
  • Lentil and chorizo dough arepas
  • Mixed dough arepa, added to sweet chili dough and dressings
  • Sweet arepa, sugar or papelón
  • Barley arepa
  • Banana arepa
  • Coroba arepa
  • Anise arepa
  • Carrot arepas
  • Arepas de Moriche
  • among others.
1

Mix the ingredients. Place the 2 cups of cornmeal and a pinch of salt in a large bowl. Use your fingers or a wooden paddle to stir both ingredients well. Measure 2 cups of hot water and gradually mix it into the flour.

  • You can buy precooked cornmeal, like P.A.N. flour, which is the most used in Venezuela. This flour does not contain gluten or preservatives and you can find it in white or yellow.
  • If you can't find cornmeal, substitute whole wheat flour or regular flour.

2

Knead the mixture. Use your hands to knead the flour and water until you create a soft but consistent dough. Knead it until it is smooth and you can turn it without sticking to your hands or the bowl.

  • If the dough is too wet, add flour a little at a time and continue to knead.
  • If the dough becomes crumbly or falls apart, add a little water. Do this very little by little until the dough has the correct texture.

3

Shape the arepas. Take a handful of the dough and roll it into a ball. Then he presses the palms of her hands into a hamburger shape. It should be about 1/2 inch (2 cm) thick and 3 to 4 inches (7.5 to 10 cm) cm in diameter. Put the first arepa on a baking tray and continue making the others.

  • If you want to save the arepas to cook later, you can cover them with plastic wrap and store them in the refrigerator for 3 or 4 more days.
  • You can also freeze them before cooking. You should wrap them in plastic one by one and place them in a sealed container. This way you can cook them up to several months later.

4

To cook them Venezuelan-style, heat the oven to 480 °F (250 °C). Do it when you are ready to cook the arepas, before frying them. The oven must be hot before putting them inside.

5

Fry the arepas Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large skillet over medium high heat. When the oil is hot enough, put several arepas in the pan and fry them until one side is browned, about 3-4 minutes. Flip them when you see a dark golden crust. Then, put them on a baking tray and continue frying the other arepas.

6

Open the arepas. Use a knife to open the arepas in half so you can fill them.
